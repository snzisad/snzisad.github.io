About

Sharif Noor Zisad is a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Alabama at Birmingham, where he holds the UAB Blazer Fellowship. His research sits at the crossroads of artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and healthcare data systems — developing frameworks that make medical environments safer, more private, and more trustworthy.

Before UAB, Sharif completed a competitive European Union–funded Erasmus Mundus Joint Master's in Green Networking and Cloud Computing, studying across France, the United Kingdom, and Sweden. He holds a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of Chittagong.

Alongside academic research, Sharif has hands-on experience building production software: automated clinical data pipelines, web platforms, analytics dashboards, and AI-powered security tools. He has also served as a guest lecturer and teaching assistant at UAB, mentoring students in networking, cloud computing, and systems design.

Research Interests

AI-Driven Cybersecurity

Security frameworks for healthcare infrastructure using LLMs, agentic AI, and threat modeling.

Healthcare IoT & Medical Data

Security and privacy of medical devices, clinical data pipelines, and EHR systems.

Privacy-Aware Access Control

Provenance-based and LLM-explainable access control for sensitive multi-stakeholder environments.

Edge Computing & IoT Security

Lightweight intrusion detection systems and secure deployment on resource-constrained devices.
